He reports no symptoms. Blood pressure is 142/86 mm Hg and pulse is 78/min. BMI is 30 kg/m2. Physical examination shows no abnormalities. Laboratory testing shows uncontrolled diabetes mellitus with diabetic nephropathy.     • Explanation

    This patient's decreased aldosterone effect on the kidneys is likely due to impaired renin secretion in the setting of juxtaglomerular apparatus destruction from diabetic nephropathy, with a downstream decrease in aldosterone production. Aldosterone acts on the nephron collecting duct to stimulate sodium reabsorption, with a consequent increase in the excretion of potassium and hydrogen (H+ is excreted in the form of ammonium [NH4+]).

    With decreased aldosterone activity, potassium and H+ excretion is decreased, leading to hyperkalemia and metabolic acidosis. The metabolic acidosis created is nonanion gap due to the retention of H+ and a net loss of bicarbonate (HCO3-). Sodium reabsorption is decreased, leading to some degree of volume depletion (total body sodium is decreased), but hyponatremia often does not occur because antidiuretic hormone levels appropriately adjust to maintain a normal serum sodium concentration.

    A reduced aldosterone effect on the kidneys and the resulting metabolic abnormalities are referred to as type 4 renal tubular acidosis (RTA). In addition to diabetes mellitus, other causes of type 4 RTA include primary aldosterone deficiency and pharmacologic aldosterone inhibition such as that induced by mineralocorticoid antagonists (eg, spironolactone, eplerenone) and ENaC channel inhibitors (eg, amiloride, triamterene).

    Educational objective:

    Type 4 renal tubular acidosis (RTA) results from a reduced aldosterone effect on the kidneys and is characterized by hyperkalemia (reduced potassium excretion) and nonanion gap metabolic acidosis (reduced hydrogen excretion in the form of ammonium). Sodium reabsorption is decreased, leading to decreased total body sodium, but the serum sodium concentration is often unchanged due to the appropriate function of antidiuretic hormone.
