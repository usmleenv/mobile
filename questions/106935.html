Researchers are studying the mechanisms that lead to acute kidney injury in septic shock. In an experiment, the renal perfusion pressure of laboratory mice is reduced to assess changes in renal tubular function and morphology. Perfusion pressure is decreased until renal tubular dysfunction occurs due to sublethal cellular ischemia; recovery of function is achieved within several hours of reperfusion. Which of the following is most likely the earliest change to occur within the renal tubules during the ischemic period?

A. Activation of cytoplasmic caspases
B. Damage to the tubular basement membrane
C. Loss of epithelial cell polarity
D. Necrosis and sloughing of epithelial cells

    • Explanation

    The mice in this experimental study are observed to have rapidly reversible renal tubular dysfunction after exposure to sublethal ischemia, which is consistent with acute tubular necrosis (ATN). Loss of epithelial cell polarity occurs early in ATN due to alterations in the actin cytoskeleton. The actin cytoskeleton is very sensitive to depletion of ATP; therefore, ischemia causes rapid cytoskeletal disruption, resulting in:

    • Blunting of apical microvilli, which reduces the surface area available for solute transport
    • Loss of cell-cell adhesion (eg, tight junctions), which causes back leakage of tubular fluid into the interstitium
    • Redistribution of integrins from the basolateral to apical membrane, which promotes cell detachment, tubular obstruction, and cast formation (by binding together detached epithelial cells)
    • Redistribution of Na-K-ATPases from the basolateral membrane to the apical membrane, which disrupts the concentration gradient necessary for sodium reabsorption

    Impaired sodium reabsorption results in sodium wasting and increases its distal delivery to the macula densa. The inappropriate juxtaglomerular feedback causes afferent arteriole constriction, which further worsens renal ischemia.

    Prolonged renal ischemia eventually leads to necrosis, further sloughing of tubular epithelial cells, and tubular obstruction; if severe enough, this can damage the basement membrane and cause tubular rupture (Choices B and D). Restoration of perfusion to cells exposed to sublethal injury allows for cellular polarity normalization and recovery of renal function.

    (Choice A) Cellular apoptosis is driven by activation of cytoplasmic caspases in response to increased mitochondrial permeability. However, this occurs following prolonged, lethal ischemia rather than the short period of sublethal ischemia in this study.

    Educational objective:

    Loss of epithelial cell polarity is an early change that occurs in renal tubular cells in response to ischemia (ie, acute tubular necrosis). Depletion of ATP causes rapid cytoskeletal disruption; this leads to a loss of cell-cell adhesion and causes redistribution of integrins and Na-K-ATPases from the basolateral membrane to the apical membrane.
