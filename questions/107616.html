    • Explanation

    Arterial blood gas analysis can be particularly helpful in the evaluation of encephalopathy (eg, confusion, decreased arousal) when a history cannot be obtained. If present, acid-base abnormalities can help guide further diagnosis and management. Acid-base status is best evaluated in a stepwise fashion:

    1. Evaluate the pH: This patient's low pH of 7.25 indicates acidemia (ie, <7.35).
    1. Identify the primary process: Analyze the metabolic component of acid-base balance, serum HCO3‚àí (normal: 24 mEq/L), and the respiratory component, PaCO2 (normal: 40 mm Hg). The parameter shifted in the direction that explains the change in pH represents the primary disorder. This patient's serum HCO3‚àí of 12 mEq/L is low (shifted toward acidity) (Choice B), indicating primary metabolic acidosis.
    1. Determine the compensation: Primary metabolic disorders are quickly compensated via adjustments in alveolar ventilation and CO2 removal (eg, hyperventilation in this patient). The expected compensation for this patient's primary metabolic acidosis is a secondary respiratory alkalosis with a PaCO2 of 24-28 mm Hg (estimated using Winters formula). This patient's PaCO2 of 28 mm Hg is within the expected range, indicating that there is no additional primary respiratory disorder (ie, appropriate compensation).

    This patient therefore has a primary metabolic acidosis without an additional primary disorder. The next step in further characterizing metabolic acidosis is calculation of the anion gap, which consists of unmeasured anions that are normally present in the serum. The anion gap is approximately equal to (Na+ ‚àí [HCO3‚àí + Cl‚àí]); this patient's anion gap of 34 mEq/L is significantly higher than normal (ie, 10-14 mEq/L) (Choice C). Because of the large number of etiologies of high anion gap metabolic acidosis (eg, toxic alcohol ingestion), further work-up is often necessary.

    (Choice D) Although primary respiratory acidosis would also cause a pH <7.35, a high PaCO2 with a compensatory rise in HCO3‚àí would be expected.

    (Choice E) Primary respiratory alkalosis is characterized by a pH >7.45 (ie, alkalemia), rather than acidemia.

    Educational objective:

    Primary metabolic acidosis is characterized by a pH <7.35 (ie, acidemia), low serum HCO3‚àí, and a compensatory low PaCO2. An anion gap (Na+ ‚àí [HCO3‚àí + Cl‚àí]) above normal (ie, 10-14 mEq/L) indicates a high anion gap metabolic acidosis.
