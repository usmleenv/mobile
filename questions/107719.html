Researchers are studying embryologic development of the eye. They observe reciprocal signaling interactions between the optic vesicle and surface ectoderm. The optic vesicle sends inductive signals to the surface ectoderm, resulting in its development into a lens. Reciprocally, the developing lens sends inductive signals to the optic vesicle, resulting in its development into the optic cup, which contributes to the retina. The differentiation of either structure cannot occur without this reciprocal signaling interaction. This type of embryologic signaling interaction also occurs between which of the following?

A. Endoderm and splanchnic mesoderm during vasculogenesis
B. Metanephric blastema and ureteric bud during kidney formation
C. Neural crest and endoderm during gut tubulogenesis
D. Sertoli cells and primordial germ cells during gamete development

    • Explanation

    Organogenesis relies on coordinated embryologic signaling between tissues for normal development. Reciprocal induction is a type of signaling that occurs between two tissues; both must be present to induce normal development.

    Eye development is an example of reciprocal induction, in which signaling molecules from the optic vesicle bind receptors on the surface ectoderm, which induces lens formation via various intracellular signal transduction pathways. The developing lens then acts as the inducer by signaling development of the optic vesicle into the optic cup.

    Reciprocal induction also occurs between the metanephric blastema and the ureteric bud, which are embryologic structures that give rise to the mature kidneys and collecting system, respectively:

    • The metanephric blastema induces formation of the ureteric bud, which then induces the metanephric blastema to condense and epithelialize.
    • In sequential iterations, the metanephric blastema then induces the ureteric bud to branch and elongate into the renal collecting system and ureters, whereas the ureteric bud induces formation of segmented nephrons, ultimately forming mature renal parenchyma.

    Normal kidney and urinary tract development depends on this reciprocal interaction because neither the metanephric blastema nor the ureteric bud would develop normally if isolated from the other (eg, defects in ureteric bud branching can result in renal agenesis).

    (Choice A) The endoderm induces splanchnic mesoderm development into primitive endothelial cells that join together to form blood vessels; however, no reciprocal signaling exists from the splanchnic mesoderm to the endoderm.

    (Choice C) Neural crest cells differentiate into enteric ganglia in response to signaling from the foregut, but the foregut develops into the intestines independent of neural crest cells. For example, Hirschsprung disease is a disorder of neural crest cell migration characterized by a segment of aganglionic colon, although the intestine itself develops normally otherwise.

    (Choice D) Sertoli cells in the fetal testis signal primordial germ cells to develop into mature gametes (ie, sperm), but no reciprocal signaling exists from germ cells to Sertoli cells.

    Educational objective:

    Reciprocal induction is a type of signaling that occurs between two tissues that requires both be present to induce normal development. Reciprocal induction occurs in development of the kidney (ie, between metanephric blastema and ureteric bud) and the eye (ie, between optic vesicle and surface ectoderm).
