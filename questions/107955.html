A 12-month-old girl is brought to the clinic for evaluation of pain. Over the last few weeks, the parents have noticed that the patient tends to cry and appear uncomfortable when she sits down. More recently, the patient has started to cry when the parents try to put her in her car seat. Examination shows a firm, tender mass along the coccyx region. Imaging reveals a tumor in the sacrococcygeal area. The mass is excised, revealing the presence of hair, teeth, skeletal muscle, and intestinal epithelium. This patient's tumor most likely originated from the same type of embryonic cell as which of the following malignancies?

A. Melanoma
B. Neuroblastoma
C. Ovarian germ cell tumor
D. Ovarian granulosa cell tumor
E. Sertoli-Leydig cell tumor

    • Explanation

    This patient's sacrococcygeal tumor contains derivatives of the ectoderm (eg, hair, teeth), mesoderm (eg, skeletal muscle), and endoderm (eg, intestinal epithelium), findings suggestive of a teratoma. Teratomas contain differentiated tissues from each of the 3 primary germ cell layers and are a type of germ cell tumor (GCT), the origin of which is the primordial germ cell.

    During early embryogenesis, the epiblast cell layer gives rise to the ectoderm, mesoderm, and endoderm in a process called gastrulation. Prior to gastrulation, however, a subset of pluripotent epiblast cells (primordial germ cells) migrates toward the genital ridge, the precursor for the undifferentiated gonad. The cells ultimately differentiate within the gonad (ovary or testis) into gametes (egg or sperm).

    Failed migration of a subpopulation of primordial germ cells can result in an extragonadal GCT, often in a midline location within the suprasellar, mediastinal, and sacrococcygeal regions; a sacrococcygeal mass can present with bowel/bladder obstruction, weakness, or pain, as seen in this patient. However, the most common location for a GCT is the gonad itself (eg, ovarian germ cell tumor); tumor subtypes include teratomas, dysgerminomas, and yolk sac tumors.

    (Choices A and B) Melanoma is a malignancy of melanocytes, and neuroblastoma is a malignancy of sympathetic ganglion cells. Melanocytes and sympathetic ganglion cells are derivatives of the neural crest, a group of multipotent, migratory cells that originate in the ectoderm germ layer.

    (Choices D and E) Ovarian granulosa cell and Sertoli-Leydig cell tumors are sex cord‚Äìstromal tumors of the ovary and testis, respectively. The stroma and sex cords are supportive tissues within the gonad that are derived from mesoderm and do not contain germ cells.
