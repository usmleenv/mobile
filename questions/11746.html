A 28-year-old man is hospitalized due to acute onset of depressed mood and suicidal ideation. The patient states he has "no way out" and has had thoughts of killing himself for the past few days. He also reports fatigue and vivid, disturbing dreams. The nursing staff note that he is irritable, withdrawn, hypersomnolent, and hyperphagic. The patient refuses to provide a urine sample. Review of his medical chart reveals an extensive history of recreational drug use and a previous hospitalization for alcohol detoxification. Blood pressure is 110/80 mm Hg and pulse is 64/min. Physical examination shows multiple, well-healed scars from a motorcycle collision but no other abnormalities. On mental status examination, he appears lethargic and dysphoric. This patient's current symptoms are most likely the result of which of the following conditions?

A. Alcohol withdrawal
B. Benzodiazepine withdrawal
C. Cannabis withdrawal
D. Cocaine withdrawal
E. Major depressive disorder
F. Opiate withdrawal

    This patient's low mood, fatigue, hypersomnia, hyperphagia, and vivid dreams are characteristic of cocaine withdrawal. Symptoms occur within hours to days of cessation or reduction of heavy or prolonged use. Following a binge, patients may experience a period of acute, intense symptoms, including severe depression with suicidal ideation and pronounced lack of energy (the "crash") along with heightened drug cravings. In contrast to withdrawal from opiates, alcohol, and benzodiazepines, withdrawal from cocaine and other stimulants usually results in minor physical symptoms. As symptoms are transient, treatment is focused primarily on supportive interventions and safety management for those with suicidal ideation.

    (Choices A and B) Alcohol withdrawal is a medically serious syndrome characterized by initial symptoms of agitation, tremors, tachycardia, and hypertension (12-24 hours), with some patients progressing to alcoholic hallucinosis, seizures, and delirium tremens. Benzodiazepine withdrawal is a medically serious syndrome similar to alcohol withdrawal given the shared GABAergic mechanism of action.

    (Choice C) Patients who cease heavy and prolonged cannabis use may have irritability, anxiety, depressed mood, and vivid dreams. However, this is accompanied by insomnia and decreased appetite (rather than hypersomnia and increased appetite). Nonspecific physical symptoms such as abdominal pain may also be present.

    (Choice E) Major depressive disorder can present with depressed mood, suicidal ideation, fatigue, irritability, social isolation, and increased sleep and appetite. However, symptoms are required for ‚â•2 weeks (rather than the past few days) and should not be better attributed to the effects of a substance known to cause such symptoms (eg, cocaine, methamphetamine withdrawal).

    (Choice F) Opiate withdrawal is characterized by abdominal cramps, nausea, vomiting, yawning, muscle aches, piloerection, lacrimation, and dilated pupils. Although withdrawal is uncomfortable, it is generally not life-threatening.

    Educational objective:

    Cocaine withdrawal is characterized by the development of acute depression accompanied by fatigue, hypersomnia, hyperphagia, and vivid dreams.
