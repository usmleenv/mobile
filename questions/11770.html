    This patient‚Äîwho has headaches, visual (eg, intermittent blurred vision) and muscular (eg, shoulder and hip stiffness) symptoms, an enlarged and tender temporal artery, and an elevated erythrocyte sedimentation rate‚Äîhas the typical features of giant cell arteritis (GCA) with associated polymyalgia rheumatica. GCA is a common systemic vasculitis that occurs almost exclusively in patients age >50. It affects medium to large arteries, particularly those in the head and neck, such as the temporal artery (ie, temporal arteritis).

    The inflammatory infiltrate in affected vessels is typically granulomatous, composed of lymphocytes (predominantly CD4-positive T cells) and macrophages, which may fuse to form multinucleated giant cells. This inflammatory reaction causes vessel damage (eg, elastic fiber fragmentation) and remodeling (eg, intimal hyperplasia) and can ultimately lead to vascular occlusion and tissue ischemia (eg, irreversible vision loss).

    The production of cytokines, in particular IL-6, appears to closely correlate with the severity of the disease; a monoclonal antibody against the IL-6 receptor (tocilizumab) is effective in treating GCA.

    (Choices A and B) The pathogenesis of antineutrophil cytoplasmic antibody (ANCA)‚Äìassociated vasculitides involves antibodies to myeloperoxidase (eg, microscopic polyangiitis) and proteinase-3 (eg, granulomatosis with polyangiitis).

    (Choice C) B cell‚Äìactivating factor (BAFF) is a cytokine belonging to the tumor necrosis factor ligand family. Inadequate levels of BAFF lead to immunodeficiency, whereas excess levels can cause autoimmune diseases (eg, systemic lupus erythematosus).

    (Choice E) IL-10 has key anti-inflammatory and immunomodulatory properties. Defective IL-10 signaling is associated with early-onset inflammatory bowel disease.

    Educational objective:

    Cell-mediated immunity is the primary mechanism underlying giant cell arteritis. The production of cytokines, in particular IL-6, is an important driver of this process and closely correlates with the severity of symptoms.
