A 42-year-old man comes to the office for a checkup. He has no medical symptoms but says, "I'm just feeling stressed and not like myself." The patient is going through a contentious divorce. He is outraged that his wife is pursuing full custody of their children and says, "I can't believe she is doing this to me." The patient works as a customer-service agent processing refund claims, which has been a welcome distraction from his upsetting personal life. He has been taking extra shifts so that he can deny as many refunds to customers as possible. Which of the following is the best explanation for this patient's behavior toward customers?

A. Acting out
B. Displacement
C. Projection
D. Rationalization
E. Reaction formation
F. Sublimation

    This patient's redirection of his anger from his wife to the customers at work is an example of the defense mechanism displacement. In displacement, emotions are unconsciously transferred from their actual target and expressed toward someone or something experienced as less threatening. In this case, the patient is angry with his wife but cannot express it directly because it may impact the child custody proceedings. Instead, he unconsciously transfers the emotion to customers, where he can express his anger through denial of refunds.

    (Choice A) Acting out refers to the expression of unacceptable thoughts or impulses through actions (eg, if this patient were to react to his wife's pursuit of full custody by vandalizing her car).

    (Choice C) In projection, a person attributes unconscious feelings to someone else (eg, if this patient were to deny he is angry and claim that it is his wife who is upset).

    (Choice D) Rationalization refers to making excuses to justify one's behavior (eg, if this patient were to justify his behavior at work by saying that all customers are untruthful and therefore deserve having their refunds denied).

    (Choice E) Reaction formation involves transforming unacceptable feelings into their opposite (eg, if this patient, who is angry at his wife, were to act in an overly loving manner toward her).

    (Choice F) Sublimation involves transforming unacceptable impulses into a socially acceptable release (eg, if this patient were to take up boxing as a hobby to vent his frustration).

    Educational objective:

    In displacement, emotions are transferred from the person causing the negative emotions and expressed toward a more neutral, less threatening person or object.
