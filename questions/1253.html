    This patient started multidrug therapy for pulmonary tuberculosis and subsequently developed constitutional symptoms, myalgias, arthralgias, and pleuritic chest pain (indicating possible serositis). The presence of antihistone antibodies suggests drug-induced lupus erythematosus (DILE).

    Unlike patients with classic lupus erythematosus, patients with DILE usually have no cutaneous (eg, malar rash), renal (eg, glomerulonephritis), or neurologic complications; in addition, laboratory evaluation usually shows no anti‚Äìdouble stranded DNA antibodies. Most cases of DILE improve rapidly when the inciting agent is discontinued.

    The drug that triggered DILE in this patient was likely isoniazid (a common component of multidrug therapy for TB), which is metabolized via phase II acetylation in the liver. Hepatic expression of N-acetyltransferase is genetically determined, and patients with a slow acetylator phenotype are at greatest risk for DILE. These patients are also predisposed to isoniazid-induced peripheral neuropathy due to retention of the drug over time, leading to elevated drug levels. Procainamide and hydralazine are also metabolized via acetylation in the liver; therefore, patients with slow acetylator phenotype can also develop DILE from these medications.

    (Choice B) Liver hydrolysis is a phase I reaction in which a compound is cleaved by adding water (eg, esterase, amidase enzymes). Phase I metabolism usually precedes phase II conjugation metabolism.

    (Choice C) Liver hydroxylation is a phase I oxidation transformation catalyzed by the cytochrome P-450 monooxygenase system. The addition of a hydroxyl group reduces lipid solubility and facilitates excretion.

    (Choice D) Liver sulfate conjugation is a phase II metabolic pathway that biotransforms drugs into more polar compounds that are more water soluble and easily excreted.

    (Choice E) Certain drugs are hydrolyzed by plasma esterases, which rapidly cleave ester linkages after the drug enters the circulation. This rapid inactivation allows for the short duration of action of these medications. Many drugs used in anesthesia (eg, succinylcholine, tetracaine, remifentanil) are metabolized by plasma hydrolysis.

    (Choice F) A few drugs are not metabolized in the body. They are excreted, mostly unchanged, in the urine and are still pharmacologically active.

    Educational objective:

    Drug-induced lupus erythematosus (DILE) is characterized by abrupt-onset lupus symptoms (eg, fever, arthralgia, pleuritis) with positive antihistone antibodies. It has been linked to drugs metabolized by N-acetylation in the liver (eg, procainamide, hydralazine, isoniazid). Genetically predisposed individuals with slow acetylator phenotype are at greater risk for DILE.
