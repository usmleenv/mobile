A medical student just finished the USMLE Step 1 examination and is anxious about her performance. She is especially unnerved when fellow students talk about the difficulty of the examination and discuss their answers to certain questions. The student decides not to think about the examination until her score arrives because "worrying isn't going to change the result." She is delighted when she receives a passing score several weeks later. Before receiving the result, the student exhibited which of the following defense mechanisms?

A. Displacement
B. Intellectualization
C. Isolation of affect
D. Rationalization
E. Repression
F. Suppression

    Defense mechanisms are means of protecting oneself from uncomfortable feelings or thoughts. Immature defense mechanisms (eg, denial, acting out, splitting) are maladaptive. They are commonly seen in children or can manifest when a person is under stress, such as this medical student. Mature defense mechanisms (eg, sublimation) allow for a healthy, adaptive response to emotional distress.

    This student is using suppression to alleviate anxiety about her examination results because she is aware of her discomfort but decides not to attend to it. Suppression is a mature defense mechanism in which an individual consciously puts aside unpleasant thoughts or feelings to better cope with the present reality. Suppression should not be confused with repression, in which unacceptable thoughts or feelings are blocked from entering conscious awareness (eg, an adult who was sexually abused as a child unconsciously buries feelings about the trauma) (Choice E).

    (Choice A) In displacement, a person transfers unpleasant feelings experienced for one person onto another less threatening person (eg, if this student had failed an examination and then went home and shouted at her spouse instead of expressing anger toward her teacher).

    (Choice B) Intellectualization involves focusing on nonemotional components of a distressing event through excessive use of abstract thinking to make the event more tolerable (eg, if this student had become preoccupied with thinking about the passing rates and other statistics of the examination).

    (Choice C) In isolation of affect, painful emotions are separated from the related event and hidden from conscious awareness (eg, if the student had remembered failing an examination but has no associated feelings about it).

    (Choice D) Rationalization involves making excuses to avoid uncomfortable truths or feelings (eg, if this student had performed poorly on the examination because she did not understand the material but then blamed her performance on poorly worded questions). In contrast, this student does not avoid her feelings or make excuses but consciously acknowledges her distress, then temporarily puts it aside until the right time to process it.

    Educational objective:

    Suppression is a mature defense mechanism involving a conscious choice not to dwell on a particular thought or feeling.
