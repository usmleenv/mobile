A 14-year-old boy is brought to the office by his parents, who are worried about his reaction to their recent decision to divorce. Despite their efforts to be supportive and engage him, he has not directly expressed any feelings about the impending divorce. When the patient is evaluated alone, he reports that he is "fine." When the physician asks if he would like to talk to a therapist about his parents' divorce, he becomes agitated and says, "No, I don't. Why is everyone so angry with me? I haven't done anything wrong." This patient is most likely using which of the following defense mechanisms?

A. Acting out
B. Displacement
C. Identification
D. Projection
E. Reaction formation
F. Regression

    Projection is a defense mechanism that involves attributing one's own uncomfortable thoughts or feelings to another person to avoid acknowledging them within oneself. This patient is likely angry with his parents about the divorce. Due to his inability to tolerate the anger within himself, he assigns the uncomfortable feeling to others around him ("Why is everyone so angry?") and perceives that his parents and the physician are angry with him, although there is little evidence that they feel this way. Projection commonly occurs in patients who lack insight into their own motivations and feelings.

    (Choice A) Acting out refers to expressing unacceptable thoughts or impulses through actions (eg, if this patient were to express his anger by destroying his parents' wedding photographs).

    (Choice B) Displacement involves redirecting unacceptable thoughts, feelings, or impulses associated with one object to another object that has less potential for a threatening response (eg, if instead of expressing anger toward his parents, who could punish him, this patient were to start kicking the defenseless family dog).

    (Choice C) Identification refers to modeling one's behavior after someone who is perceived to be more powerful or prestigious. A classic example is the child of an abusive father who becomes a child abuser himself.

    (Choice E) Reaction formation is the transformation of an unacceptable feeling to its opposite (eg, if instead of expressing anger, this patient were to behave in an overly affectionate way toward his parents and exhibit enthusiastic support for their decision to divorce).

    (Choice F) Regression refers to reverting to a less age-appropriate way of coping with difficulties (eg, if this patient were to begin sleeping in his parents' room due to anxiety about being alone, which is more characteristic of an earlier developmental stage).

    Educational objective:

    Projection is a defense mechanism involving the attribution of one's unacceptable feelings or thoughts to another person who does not actually have them.
