A 45-year-old woman brings her 5-year-old stepson to a cognitive-behavioral therapy session for attention deficit hyperactivity disorder. In the waiting room, the woman seems excessively concerned when the boy accidentally trips and is clearly uninjured. The therapist later checks in with the woman alone, who says that she never wanted children due to her unhappy childhood and wishes her marriage did not include being a stepmother to a 5-year-old boy. When the boy enters the room, the woman tells the therapist how "brilliant and smart" the child is and describes the elaborate preparations she is making for his birthday party. Which of the following defense mechanisms best explains this woman's behavior?

A. Displacement
B. Projection
C. Rationalization
D. Reaction formation
E. Splitting
F. Suppression

    Reaction formation is a defense mechanism in which an individual transforms unacceptable feelings or impulses into their extreme opposites. This woman does not want to be a stepmother, and her resentment toward the child is masked with declarations of love. In addition, her negative feelings toward the child are transformed into oversolicitous behavior (in response to his tripping), excessive praise, and elaborate birthday party preparations.

    (Choice A) Displacement is the inappropriate transfer of feelings or impulses toward a less threatening object (eg, if this woman yelled at her dog instead of the child).

    (Choice B) Projection involves attributing one's own unacceptable impulses or behavior to another person or situation (eg, if this woman accused her husband of disliking the child).

    (Choice C) In rationalization, an individual makes excuses for unacceptable feelings or behavior (eg, if this woman neglected the child and explained that she is much too old to be caring for him).

    (Choice E) Splitting is an immature defense mechanism in which an individual is unable to integrate mixed feelings (eg, if this woman alternately felt the boy was "all bad" or "all good"). It is commonly seen in borderline personality disorder.

    (Choice F) Suppression is a conscious decision not to attend to unpleasant thoughts or feelings as a way to cope with reality (eg, if this woman acknowledged her resentment toward the child but chose to put it aside for the sake of her marriage and tended to the child's needs appropriately). It would not entail overcompensating for her feelings with extreme praise or oversolicitous behavior.

    Educational objective:

    Reaction formation is a defense mechanism that involves replacing unacceptable feelings and impulses with their extreme opposites.
