Researchers working at a national foundation for prematurity and birth defects are investigating the physiologic processes that occur during embryonic kidney development. Their research focuses on the inductive signals exchanged between the metanephric diverticulum and metanephric blastema that drive their differentiation into tissues forming the mature kidney. The researchers develop a method to reproduce the inductive signal that normally comes from the metanephric diverticulum. After applying this inductive signal to the metanephric blastema, which of the following adult derivatives will most likely develop?

A. Collecting ducts
B. Distal convoluted tubules
C. Major calyces
D. Minor calyces
E. Renal pelvis

    • Explanation

    Embryonic kidney development begins with the formation of the nephrogenic cord (derived from the urogenital ridge, or intermediate mesoderm). The cord sequentially forms 3 sets of nephric systems: pronephros, mesonephros, and metanephros.

    The rudimentary pronephros forms first and later completely regresses. The mesonephros forms next from the midportion of the nephrogenic cord; in females, it regresses, whereas in males it persists as the wolffian ducts, forming the ductus deferens and epididymis.

    The metanephros forms last from the caudal end of the nephrogenic cord and persists as the mature upper urinary tract. Development of the metanephros begins with formation of the metanephric diverticulum (ureteric bud), which penetrates the sacral intermediate mesoderm to induce the formation of the metanephric blastema. The reciprocal exchange of inductive signals between the ureteric bud and metanephric blastema drives their differentiation into the structures that form the upper urinary tract.

    • Metanephric blastema: This structure gives rise to the glomeruli, Bowman space, proximal tubules, loop of Henle, and distal convoluted tubules.
    • Metanephric diverticulum (ureteric bud): This structure gives rise to the collecting system of the kidney, including the collecting tubules and ducts, major and minor calyces, renal pelvis, and ureters (Choices A, C, D, and E).

    Educational objective:

    The mature upper urinary tract is derived from the metanephros, which comprises the metanephric blastema and metanephric diverticulum (ureteric bud). The metanephric blastema gives rise to the glomeruli, Bowman space, proximal tubules, loop of Henle, and distal convoluted tubules. The ureteric bud becomes the collecting system of the kidney (eg, collecting tubules and ducts, major and minor calyces, renal pelvis, ureters).
