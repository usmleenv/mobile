    (Choices A and B) Anion gap metabolic acidosis results from the addition of unmeasured acidic compounds to the blood. The compounds donate H+ to bind up serum HCO3‚àí, reducing buffering capacity and causing metabolic acidosis. The remaining anionic component increases the anion gap. Common etiologies of anion gap metabolic acidosis include increased production of ketones (eg, acetoacetate, beta-hydroxybutyrate), which occurs with diabetic ketoacidosis, and increased production of lactic acid, which occurs with reduced organ and tissue perfusion (eg, sepsis).
