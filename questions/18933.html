    This patient likely has nonanion gap metabolic acidosis (NAGMA) due to infusion of excess normal saline. Excess sodium chloride increases serum chloride (Cl‚àí) to cause hyperchloremia. Because Cl‚àí and bicarbonate (HCO3‚àí) are the predominant anions in the body, the increased serum Cl‚àí causes intracellular shifting of HCO3‚àí to maintain electronegative balance. This "loss" of HCO3‚àí (reduced serum HCO3‚àí) decreases blood pH. Infusion of excess normal saline also increases intravascular volume, which the kidneys respond to by increasing sodium (Na+) excretion, resulting in increased urine Na+.
