A 48-year-old man begins psychotherapy due to depression and escalating conflicts with his boss. He makes progress in the therapy sessions and reports feeling less depressed. The patient has also reflected on how his father was rarely available during his childhood and prioritized work over spending time with him. The following week, the psychiatrist unexpectedly cancels a session to attend to a family emergency. At the next appointment, the psychiatrist apologizes for canceling the last session. The patient replies, "You aren't there when I need you because I'm not important to you." Which of the following is the most likely explanation for this patient's reaction to the psychiatrist?

A. Acting out
B. Displacement
C. Projection
D. Reaction formation
E. Transference

    • Explanation

    Transference is the unconscious shifting of emotions or desires associated with a person from the past to another person in the present. Transference often originates from early attachment figures, especially parents. It includes reactivation of past emotions, thoughts, and behaviors through current interactions.

    In this scenario, the psychiatrist unexpectedly cancels the session for an understandable reason. However, the patient's feelings of childhood abandonment related to his unavailable father are unconsciously reactivated and reexperienced during the treatment session ("You aren't there when I need you because I'm not important to you").

    In contrast to transference, countertransference is the physician's own feelings and thoughts about the patient, recreated in the treatment setting, based on the physician's past experiences.

    (Choice A) In acting out, unconscious feelings or impulses are expressed through inappropriate behaviors (eg, if this patient had impulsively broken items in the psychiatrist's office due to rage at his perceived abandonment).

    (Choice B) Displacement involves unconscious redirection of strong negative emotions toward an object that is less threatening (eg, if this patient never recognized his anger at his father and screamed at his children instead).

    (Choice C) Projection involves misattributing one's own unacceptable feelings to another person (eg, if the patient, unable to acknowledge his disappointment toward his psychiatrist about canceling, had instead perceived his psychiatrist as being disappointed with him).

    (Choice D) In reaction formation, uncomfortable feelings are transformed into their opposites (eg, if this patient had expressed deep appreciation for the psychiatrist's care rather than acknowledging his disappointment).

    Educational objective:

    Transference is the unconscious recreation and experience of emotions, thoughts, and behaviors associated with past relationships. It can be negative or positive and influences patient interactions in physician-patient relationships.
