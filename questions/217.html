A 21-year-old man comes to clinic for a routine health maintenance visit. During testicular examination, he is found to have a single testis in the scrotum. Laboratory evaluation shows an elevated serum FSH level and a normal serum LH level. Production of which of the following substances is most likely decreased in this patient?

A. Dehydroepiandrosterone
B. Dihydrotestosterone
C. GnRH
D. Inhibin B
E. Testosterone

    • Explanation

    Normal functioning of the hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis involves pulsatile secretion of GnRH from the hypothalamus, which stimulates the release of FSH and LH from the gonadotrophs of the anterior pituitary. LH triggers the release of testosterone from the Leydig cells of the testes; FSH stimulates the release of inhibin B from the Sertoli cells of the testes. Testosterone has a negative feedback effect on LH and GnRH secretion, while inhibin B suppresses FSH secretion.

    Sertoli cells within the seminiferous tubules are temperature sensitive and prone to atrophy from heat damage when located outside of the scrotum, which is at a relatively lower temperature than the body's core. Therefore, when there is an undescended testicle (as in this patient with only 1 scrotal testicle) Sertoli cell mass is reduced and circulating levels of inhibin B are low. In turn, the lack of negative feedback from inhibin B on FSH production results in elevated FSH levels.

    (Choice A) Although dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA) is a precursor to testosterone, the testicles only release a small amount of DHEA into the circulation. Systemic levels of DHEA are derived primarily from the adrenal glands.

    (Choices B, C, and E) In contrast to heat-sensitive Sertoli cells, Leydig cell function is not significantly affected by increased temperature, so testosterone, dihydrotestosterone, and LH levels are usually normal in cryptorchidism. In addition, this patient's normal LH level is indicative of normal (not decreased) GnRH activity.

    Educational objective:

    Inhibin B, which is produced by heat-sensitive Sertoli cells, is a physiologic inhibitor of FSH secretion. In patients with cryptorchidism, atrophy of Sertoli cells results in decreased inhibin B and, in turn, elevated FSH. In contrast, Leydig cell function is not significantly affected, so testosterone and LH levels are typically normal in these patients.
