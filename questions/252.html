A 16-year-old primigravida comes to the emergency department in active labor. A fetal heartbeat is undetectable, and she delivers a stillborn boy vaginally. Examination of the stillborn shows several dysmorphic features, including closely set eyes and an elongated nasal structure. A fetal autopsy reveals fused cerebral hemispheres with an absent forebrain fissure and a single intracranial ventricle. This patient's autopsy findings are most indicative of which of the following types of congenital anomalies?

A. Agenesis
B. Association
C. Deformation

    The physical features of this stillborn are consistent with holoprosencephaly, or incomplete division of the forebrain (prosencephalon) into 2 hemispheres. Cleavage of the prosencephalon into the telencephalon and diencephalon normally occurs in early gestation. Failure of cleavage at different points in division can result in an array of anomalies, ranging from mild midline defects (eg, ocular hypotelorism, cleft lip/palate) to severe defects (eg, single midline eye, primitive nasal structure or proboscis, midfacial clefts).

    Holoprosencephaly is an example of a malformation, an intrinsic defect that occurs during embryonic development and results in the abnormal formation of a body structure. Pathogenesis likely involves a combination of genetic (eg, trisomy 13, sonic hedgehog gene mutations) and environmental (eg, maternal alcohol use) factors.

    Other examples of malformations include spina bifida (failure of neural tube closure) and cleft lip/palate (failure of lip/palate fusion).

    (Choice A) Agenesis refers to the complete absence of an organ. Renal agenesis, the congenital absence of one or both kidneys, is an example of this type of abnormality.

    (Choice B) An association refers to multiple anomalies without a known unifying cause that occur together more frequently than chance alone would predict. Vertebral defects, anal atresia, cardiac defects, tracheoesophageal fistula, renal anomalies, and limb abnormalities (VACTERL) are an example of an association of anomalies without a known common etiology.

    (Choice C) Deformations are fetal structural anomalies that occur due to extrinsic mechanical forces. The pressure applied by the uterus is one of the most common deforming forces. For example, uterine constraint on a fetus (eg, breech position, uterine fibroids) can cause positional talipes equinovarus (clubfoot), a condition in which the foot is plantar flexed and internally rotated.

    (Choice D) Dysplasia refers to abnormal cellular architecture within a tissue. Osteogenesis imperfecta, a skeletal disorder characterized by brittle bones that fracture easily, occurs with dysplasia of connective tissue due to absent or abnormal type I collagen.

    Educational objective:

    A malformation refers to an abnormal body structure due to an intrinsic defect in development. Holoprosencephaly is a malformation characterized by a spectrum of fetal facial and cerebral anomalies due to incomplete division of the forebrain (prosencephalon).
