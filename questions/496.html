A 38-year-old woman comes to the emergency department due to severe abdominal pain and delivers a stillborn neonate who is missing a large portion of the scalp and skull, partially exposing the brain, which is also only partially formed. The patient did not know she was pregnant and had no prenatal care. Medical history is significant for bipolar disorder and chronic hypertension. Interview of the patient reveals pressured and tangential speech, and she states that she takes certain prescription medications when she remembers but cannot identify which ones. If used during pregnancy, which of the following substances most likely contributed to this neonate's abnormalities?

A. ACE inhibitor
B. Alcohol
C. Heroin
D. Isotretinoin
E. Lithium
F. Valproate

    • Explanation

    This stillborn neonate has anencephaly, or absence of the forebrain and major portions of the skull. Anencephaly is an open neural tube defect (NTD) caused by defective primary neurulation, an embryologic process in which the neural folds fuse in the midline to create the neural tube. Fusion starts in the cervical region and extends rostrally and caudally to the anterior and posterior neuropores, respectively. Failure of anterior (rostral) neuropore closure results in anencephaly, whereas failure of posterior (caudal) neuropore closure results in an open spinal dysraphism (eg, myelomeningocele).

    Most isolated NTDs are related to folate (vitamin B9) deficiency (eg, poor dietary intake). Folate supplementation is recommended for all women of childbearing age because the neural tube develops during the time when most women are unaware of their pregnancy.

    In addition to insufficient dietary folate, first trimester use of medications that impair folate metabolism are associated with a 10- to 20-fold increased risk of NTDs. These medications include valproate, which is typically prescribed for bipolar disorder (likely in this patient) or epilepsy. Others include methotrexate and trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole. These medications are contraindicated in pregnancy.

    (Choices A, D, and E) ACE inhibitors, isotretinoin, and lithium are contraindicated in pregnancy due to teratogenic effects. ACE inhibitors cause fetal renal damage, lithium use is associated with increased incidence of Ebstein anomaly, and isotretinoin is a potent teratogen that can cause a multitude of defects (eg, microcephaly, ear/facial deformities, cardiac defects). However, use of these substances during pregnancy is not associated with NTDs.

    (Choices B and C) Alcohol use is associated with fetal alcohol syndrome; findings include a smooth filtrum, thin upper lip, and small palpebral fissures. Heroin or other opiate use during pregnancy can lead to withdrawal in the infant postnatally (neonatal abstinence syndrome). NTDs are not associated with prenatal alcohol or opiate use.

    Educational objective:

    Failure of neural tube closure results in neural tube defects (eg, anencephaly, meningomyelocele). Folate deficiency is the greatest risk factor, and medications that impair folate metabolism (eg, valproate) are associated with an increased risk.
