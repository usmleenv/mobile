    LMN = lower motor neuron; UMN = upper motor neuron.

    This patient with muscle weakness and gait abnormalities died of pneumonia, which is a common cause of death in patients with neurodegenerative diseases. Autopsy showed loss of both upper (UMNs: corticospinal tracts) and lower motor neurons (LMNs: anterior roots of spinal cord, cranial nerve nuclei), which is consistent with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS).

    In patients with ALS, toxic intracellular protein inclusions accumulate in motor neurons and are thought to trigger apoptosis. Bunina bodies are eosinophilic cytoplasmic inclusions that are periodic acid‚ÄìSchiff positive and often seen in remaining neurons. Apoptotic neurons are then replaced by glial cells.

    Loss of UMN fibers leads to atrophy of the precentral motor gyrus and degeneration of the corticospinal tracts, which results in increased muscle tone (stiffness) and hyperreflexia. LMNs in cranial nerve nuclei (eg, hypoglossal, ambiguus, motor trigeminal) are often affected, which contributes to the bulbar symptoms often seen in ALS. Loss of LMN fibers in the spinal cord leads to thinning of the anterior roots and neurogenic atrophy of the skeletal muscles. Respiratory failure is the most common cause of death in patients with ALS due to diaphragmatic weakness, an ineffective cough, and chronic aspiration.

    (Choice B) Friedreich ataxia shows widespread neuronal degeneration in the posterior columns, spinocerebellar tracts, cranial nerve nuclei, cerebellum, and motor cortex. In addition, it typically manifests before adulthood and is associated with hypertrophic cardiomyopathy; most patients die from arrhythmia or heart failure.

    (Choice C) Although Guillain-Barr√© syndrome (ie, acute inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy) can lead to respiratory failure, it is characterized by inflammation and demyelination of peripheral nerves (including spinal nerve roots); CNS sites, including the precentral motor gyrus and cranial nerve nuclei, remain morphologically normal.

    (Choice D) Multiple sclerosis is a demyelination disease characterized by irregularly shaped plaques in the white matter of the CNS. Although regional demyelination can occur, atrophy affecting the entire motor pathway is not seen.

    (Choice E) Vitamin B12 deficiency causes subacute combined degeneration of the spinal cord due to defective myelin formation. This is seen most prominently in the dorsal (posterior) and lateral spinal columns. Microscopically, edema of myelin layers is seen with eventual degeneration of neurons.

    Educational objective:

    Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis causes both upper and lower motor neuron degenerative lesions. Loss of upper motor neurons leads to atrophy of the precentral motor gyrus and degeneration of the corticospinal tracts. Loss of lower motor neurons leads to thinning of the anterior roots of the spinal cord and atrophy of cranial nerve motor nuclei.
